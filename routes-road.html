関東 · Kanto

Tokyo & Surrounds

Easy

多摩川サイクリングロード

48 km 2–3 hours Completely flat

Tokyo's most-loved dedicated cycling road. Follow the Tama River from Tachikawa all the way to the sea at Kawasaki — 48km of smooth, car-free tarmac through riverside parks, under cherry blossom tunnels, and past the wetlands of Kawasaki. Ideal for a first long ride or a relaxed spin with great scenery.

Hard

道志道 — Tokyo 2020 Olympic Route

138 km 5–7 hours 2,000m+ elevation

This is the route that crowned Olympic champion Richard Carapaz. Starting from Musashino Forest Park in western Tokyo, the road climbs through the deep mountain forests of Doshi valley, cresting the Fuji Five Lakes plateau with breathtaking views of Mount Fuji before finishing at the iconic Fuji International Speedway. A bucket-list ride for serious cyclists.

Moderate

三浦半島一周 — Miura Peninsula Loop

55 km 3–4 hours Rolling coastal

A classic weekend escape from Tokyo. The Miura Peninsula juts into Sagami Bay with dramatic cliffs, hidden coves, and on clear days, an unobstructed view of Mount Fuji across the water. Loop the peninsula from Yokosuka, passing Misaki tuna port and the surfer beaches of Hayama. Accessible by train from Shinagawa in under an hour.

四国 · Shikoku

Shikoku Island

Easy

しまなみ海道 — Shimanami Kaido

70 km 4–5 hours Gentle bridges

Voted one of the world's greatest cycling routes. The Shimanami Kaido connects Onomichi in Hiroshima to Imabari in Ehime via six islands and a series of spectacular suspension bridges across the Seto Inland Sea. The cycling lanes are dedicated and well-signed in English — a rare and genuinely magical experience. Lemon farms, island villages, and turquoise water all the way.

Easy

四万十川サイクリング — Shimanto River

40 km 2.5–3.5 hours Flat riverside

Japan's last pristine river. The Shimanto flows through Kochi Prefecture in near-perfect wilderness — no dams, no development, just clear water, traditional submersible bridges (chinkabashi), and deep green forest. The riverside cycling path is quiet, completely flat, and utterly serene. A complete contrast to city riding and unlike anywhere else in Japan.

九州 · Kyushu

Kyushu Island

Hard

阿蘇パノラマライン — Aso Panorama Road

75 km 4–6 hours Volcanic climbs

Cycling inside an active volcano caldera is as dramatic as it sounds. The Aso Panorama Line loops the rim of one of the world's largest calderas, with sweeping grassland views, steam venting from Mount Nakadake, and long open roads that feel like the surface of another planet. The climbs are real, but the descents and vistas make every metre worthwhile.

Moderate

由布院 → 別府 — Yufuin to Beppu

28 km 1.5–2.5 hours Downhill finish

Start in the misty onsen village of Yufuin beneath Mount Yufu, then descend through rolling countryside and bamboo forests to the steaming hot-spring city of Beppu and the coast of Beppu Bay. It's a short ride but packs in everything: mountain scenery, rural Japan, and a dramatic arrival at one of the country's most famous resort towns. Finish with a hot spring soak — you've earned it.
