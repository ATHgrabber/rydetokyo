Easy

Asakusa → Sumida → Odaiba

22 km round trip 2–3 hours Mostly flat

Start at Senso-ji Temple in Asakusa, head south along the Sumida River on dedicated cycling paths with stunning views of the Tokyo Skytree. Cross Rainbow Bridge to Odaiba — a man-made island with waterfront parks, futuristic architecture, and great coffee. Return via the same route or take a ferry back.

Moderate

Shibuya → Daikanyama → Nakameguro → Shimokitazawa

18 km 2–3 hours Some gentle hills

Tokyo's hippest neighbourhoods in one ride. Start at Shibuya Crossing, wind through the boutiques of Daikanyama, follow the cherry-blossom-lined Meguro River canal, then continue to the indie cafés and vintage shops of Shimokitazawa. The e-assist makes the gentle hills effortless — perfect for a relaxed afternoon of exploring.

Easy

Imperial Palace Loop & Marunouchi

15 km 1.5–2 hours Completely flat

The classic Tokyo ride. Circle the Imperial Palace — Tokyo's green heart — on the 5km outer loop path, then explore the gleaming glass towers of Marunouchi and the old brick buildings of the Mitsubishi Ichigokan area. Sunday mornings the roads around the Palace are partially closed to cars, making it ideal for cycling. Short, iconic, and unmissable.

Easy

Yanaka → Nezu → Ueno — Old Tokyo

12 km 2–3 hours Flat to gentle

Step back into old Edo. Yanaka is one of Tokyo's last historic shitamachi (old town) districts — traditional wooden houses, temples, narrow lanes, and a famous shopping street. Wind through to Nezu Shrine (a smaller, quieter alternative to Fushimi Inari), then finish at Ueno Park with its world-class museums and cherry trees. Best explored slowly.
