Road Rules & Parking

Ride safely.
Ride legally.

Japan has clear cycling laws. Here's what every visitor needs to know before getting on the road.

The Essentials

Key rules at a glance

🚦

Ride on the left

Japan drives on the left. Bicycles follow the same rule — always ride on the left side of the road, in the direction of traffic.

🚲

Use the road, not the pavement

In Japan, cyclists are generally required to use the road. Some pavements (marked with blue signs) allow cycling — but you must yield to pedestrians.

🔴

Obey traffic signals

Red lights apply to cyclists too. Running a red is illegal and dangerous. Treat all traffic signals and stop signs as you would in a car.

🌙

Use lights at night

Your e-bike has a front light built in. By law you must have a front white/yellow light when riding after dark. A rear reflector or red light is also required.

📱

No phones while riding

Using a smartphone while cycling is illegal and carries a fine of up to ¥100,000. Mount your phone or stop to check maps.

🎧

No headphones (both ears)

Wearing earphones in both ears while cycling is prohibited. One ear is technically fine, but we recommend none — Tokyo streets are busy.

🍺

No riding under the influence

Cycling drunk is illegal in Japan. Fines up to ¥1,000,000 and possible imprisonment. This applies equally to electric bikes.

☂️

No umbrella riding

Cycling while holding an umbrella is illegal. Use a rain jacket instead — we include one on request for rainy days.

Parking

Where to park — and where not to

Tokyo takes illegal parking seriously. Your bike can be towed and impounded within minutes if parked in the wrong place.

✅ Safe to park

  • 🅿️ Designated bicycle parking lots (駐輪場) — free or paid, very common near stations
  • 🏨 Your hotel — ask the concierge for bike storage
  • 🏬 Shops with bicycle racks — look for white painted bike symbols on the ground
  • 🌳 Parks with dedicated parking zones — Ueno, Yoyogi, Shinjuku Gyoen all have these

❌ Do not park here

  • 🚫 Pavements — unless there's a clearly marked bicycle parking zone
  • 🚫 In front of shops — even briefly, if it's not a parking zone
  • 🚫 Train station entrances — heavy enforcement, bikes towed within minutes
  • 🚫 Red/yellow curb areas — these are no-stopping zones

💡 Tip: The Google Maps app shows bicycle parking lots when you search nearby. Or just ask us on WhatsApp — we know good spots near all the major sightseeing areas.

E-Bike Specific

Rules specific to electric bikes

No licence is required for Japanese-compliant e-bikes (denki assist jitensha). Our Giant Escape R E+ is fully compliant — it provides assist only while pedalling and cuts off at 24 km/h. No registration, no number plate, no licence needed.
