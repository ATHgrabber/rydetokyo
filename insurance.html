Insurance FAQ

Common questions

Each bike comes with a quality u-lock. Always lock the bike to a fixed object when unattended. In the event of theft with the lock in use, please file a police report immediately (dial 110) and notify us right away. Theft coverage terms depend on whether the bike was properly locked — we'll guide you through the process.
If you're involved in an accident: 1) Check if anyone is injured and call 119 (ambulance) if needed. 2) Call the police (110) if there's property damage or injury to a third party — this is legally required in Japan. 3) Contact us immediately on WhatsApp. Do not leave the scene without exchanging details if a third party is involved.
Most standard travel insurance policies cover cycling as a recreational activity. However, policies vary — check for "cycling" or "bicycle activities" in your policy documents. If you're uncertain, contact your insurer before your trip. We recommend policies from major providers that explicitly include personal accident during cycling.
The following are not covered by standard insurance: damage caused while riding under the influence of alcohol or drugs, intentional damage, damage caused by using the bike in an unsafe or illegal manner, and loss of personal belongings. The optional damage protection add-on covers accidental damage but not the above exclusions.

Safety Tips

Ride smart

Always wear your helmet

We include a helmet — please use it. It's the single most effective safety measure.

Make eye contact with drivers

At intersections, always confirm that drivers have seen you before proceeding.

Test the brakes before you ride

Give the brakes a squeeze before setting off. The hydraulic disc brakes are powerful — get used to them gently first.

Signal your turns

Use hand signals to indicate turning. It's not legally required but it's good practice and keeps you safe around traffic.
